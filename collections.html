Collections

Mirrors & Tables

Designed by British artist Anthony Critchlow, the mirrors and consoles encapsulate his playful use of circles and spheres with symmetrical and asymmetrical shapes. Made from stainless steel, these pieces can be finished to any colour using PVD coatings. They are available in Polished Nickel & Warm Gold as standard.

Oysters & Pearls

Designed by British artist Anthony Critchlow, these pendants are designed for shallow ceilings or significant high spaces and are available in sizes from 30cm up to 1.5 metres as standard. They can be customised in any way and detailing added or removed according to whim. Iridescent glass spheres in 3 sizes are set into a welded random pattern of metal rings. Light patterns are cast across walls and ceilings when alight.

Plaza & Beluga

Designed by British artist Anthony Critchlow, the plaza and beluga ranges are more symmetrical and formal and are a modern nod to the elegance of the 1920’s. The collection incorporates pendants, wall sconces and table lamps. They can be customised to any size and available on timeless polished nickel and warm gold as standard.

Anenome

The Anenome series of table lamps and sculptural spheres were designed for Best & Lloyd by Anthony Crithlow, a British sculptor, artist and drummer. Each piece is lit internally and on top and throws light and reflections across the walls and surfaces. Available in three standard sizes, the lamps can also be scaled to order.

Seapod

The Seapod series of sconces were designed for Best & Lloyd by Anthony Crithlow, a British sculptor, artist and drummer. Each combines symmetry of form with an asymmetrical structure, throwing elegant light patterns across wall and surfaces.

Faceted

The Jewel & Gem collections represent a collaboration between Best & Lloyd & Bohemian Glass cutters to support a skill that was dying out in the region and to encourage the young to come into the ageing craft. The shapes echo gem stones with colours to match. A Large vase blank can weigh 15kg before cutting begins and take up to 2 hours to blow and 20 hours to cut. Glass colour rod is used initially and then the weight is built up layer by layer of molten clear glass until it reaches its pre-cut size, around double its final weight.

Ring

The ring glass is made using a complicated technique called cane work. First a large pickup of molten glass is taken from the furnace and drawn out to create a thick long piece of glass. This is then reheated and curved around a tool of the correct diameter before being cut and the 2 ends joined white still hot. Each is unique and the metal work designed according to the ring received.

Blown Drops

Blown by hand using art glass techniques each pair are made to order and can be blown in any colour. They are made by a team in bohemia using century old traditions. Fluid and textural the glass collects the light and throws interesting patterns.

Encalmo

Incalmo is the art of joining two separately worked pieces possibly in different colours that are worked simultaneously by 2 separate teams. It is technically very difficult and requires great precision. This is a small collection of table and pendant lamps that can offset two colours. Made in Britain by British artist Stewart Hearn.

Soda

The soda collection is a series of pieces made with the introduction of baking soda into the glass blowing process. The bubbles formed remain captured in the glass.

Lead Crystal

Hand blown in Tuscany by one of the few glass blowing companies still working with 24% Lead Crystal. There is a purity and depth to lead crystal that is not present in ordinary glass and we have selected a few of our favourite cut pieces to continue.

Chelsea

Canework and handblown tubes are amongst the hardest glass blowing techniques. The glass is pulled through a profiled tool either dropped as the blower pulls or else a lift is used. The skill is to stop the tube falling in on itself as it is pulled. Most tube work is mould blown with very few glassblowers left who can work the glassine of whom is English glass artist Stewart Hearn. The chelsea range features several sizes of tube including ribbed and twisted profiles. Each can be blown in any colour.

Heritage

The Heritage collection is predominantly a collection of our artisanal leatherwork with a emphasis on reading and desk lights with matching floor lamps. Widely used on Yachts, offices and bedrooms, the range focuses on practical usage. LED reading lights, sort downline desk lights and directional lighting. To enhance their function, they have been enhanced with hand worked & tooled vegetable dyed leather. They are available in 7 different leather colours and all our standard metal finishes. Any combination can be accommodated and the stitching colour can be selected to contrast or compliment.

Harman

Chris Harman is an American sculptor and ceramicist who works in Australia. The range is made from hand formed bisque porcelain air dried on wooden blocks which gives the lamps their distinct and naturalistic feel. Each piece is unique and made to order. 2019 sees the addition of several pendants to the collection each made with 400 hand rolled porcelain spheres.

Fromental

Fromental are a British company specialising in hand painted and embroidered silk wall papers. This small collection of table lights highlights the beauty of their work. The embroidery lifts the designs from the surface. The lamps are available in any of Fromental’s patterns or colourways.

Guggisberg

Philip Baldwin & Monica Guggisberg are two of the world’s foremost glass artists. Philip specialises in blowing overlay colours while Monica has an encyclopaedic knowledge of cutting techniques researched while at Orrefors in Sweden and later in Murano. Working from an idyllic studio on the Welsh English border they are best known for their vessels and boats. We feature a small collection designed for Best & Lloyd using four separate cutting techniques each peeling off a layer in of colour in different ways to reveal the one below.

Eglomise

Eglomise is the art of painting on the reverse of a panel of glass. The Eaton Table and wall lamp feature scenes from discarded 18th Century Silk wall panels originally gracing the walls of Eaton Hall in Cheshire. Painted for Best & Lloyd by Sterling Studios, the lamp chassis can be used to display any design.

Cast

This collections feature found objects and original artwork that have been modified into lamps. We use both sand and lost wax casting methods, the pieces are unique and cast from the original patterns in brass and bronze before being patinated to order.
